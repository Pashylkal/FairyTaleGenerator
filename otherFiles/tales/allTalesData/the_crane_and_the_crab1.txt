The crane and quarreled as to their powers of racing. The crab said hewould go the fastest, and that the crane might fly across from point topoint, while he went round by the shore. The crane flew off, and the crabstayed quietly in his hole, trusting to the multitude of his brethren todeceive the crane. The crane flew to the first point, and seeing a crabhole, put down his ear, and heard a buzzing noise. "That slave is herebefore me," said he, and flew on to the next point. Here the same thinghappened, till at last, on reaching a point above Serua, the crane fellexhausted, and was drowned in the sea.
