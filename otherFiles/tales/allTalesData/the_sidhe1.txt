As to their food, they will use common things leftfor them on the hearth or outside the threshold,cold potatoes it may be, or a cup of water or ofmilk. But for their feasts they choose the best ofall sorts, taking it from the solid world, leavingsome worthless likeness in its place. when theyrob the potatoes from the ridges the diggers findbut rottenness and decay. they take the strengthfrom the meat in the pot, so that when put on theplates it does not nourish. They will not touchsalt. there is danger to them in it. They will goto good cellars to bring away the wine.
